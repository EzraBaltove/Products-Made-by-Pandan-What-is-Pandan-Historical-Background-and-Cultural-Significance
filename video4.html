Pandan Sandals

Step by Step Process

1. Select mature pandanus leaves that are long, broad, and free from holes or damage. Typically,
leaves that are about 3-4 feet (1 meter) long work best.

2.Wash the leaves thoroughly to remove dirt and sap. Let them dry for a while if they are wet.

Pandanus leaves can be stiff and difficult to work with. To soften them: Rub the leaves between your hands or with a stone to loosen the fibers.
Alternatively, you can soak the leaves in water for a few hours to make them more pliable.

3. Cut the leaves into long strips. Youâ€™ll need about 6-8 strips per sandal, depending
on how thick or thin you want them. Make the strips about 1 to 2 inches wide.

4. To create the sandal base, weave pandanus leaf strips using the over-under technique.
Lay out four parallel strips with gaps and weave others over and under them to form a flat, sturdy surface.
Once it fits your foot size, secure the ends with knots or stitches.

5. To make sandal straps, cut two longer pandanus leaf strips for the main straps and two shorter
ones for the toe loop or connections. Attach the main straps to the base by folding them over the edges and securing with stitching or knots.
For the toe loop, create a simple loop or add more straps for a complex design. Use twine or strong thread to
securely stitch the straps to the base.

6. To make the sandals more comfortable and durable, you can attach a piece of leather, fabric,
or another material to the bottom of the woven base. This will serve as the "sole" of the sandal.

7. Trim any loose ends or uneven strips for a neat appearance.
If desired, you can decorate the sandals with beads, shells, or embroidery to add a personal touch.

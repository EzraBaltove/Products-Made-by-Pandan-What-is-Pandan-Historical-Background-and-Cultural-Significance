Pandan Leaf Wallet

Step by Step Process

1. Choose leaves that are long, broad, and free of holes. They should be fresh or well-dried.

2.Wash the pandan leaves thoroughly to remove dirt and sap. Leave them to dry completely if they were freshly picked.

3. Use scissors or a sharp knife to cut the leaves into long strips. The strips should be around 1 to 2
inches wide and as long as possible (about 3-4 feet, depending on your wallet size).

4. Take about 4-6 strips of pandan leaves and lay them out flat, parallel to each other. These will be the foundation of your wallet.
Continue weaving until the desired width and length of the wallet's base are achieved.
The typical wallet width is around 4-5 inches (10-12 cm), and the length should be around 7-8 inches (18-20 cm), but you can adjust according to your preference.

5. Once you've woven a rectangular base, fold the woven piece in half to create the main body of the wallet.

6. If you'd like to add internal pockets for cards, cut smaller strips of pandan leaves to weave additional compartments inside the wallet

Pandan Leaf Mat or “Banig”

Step by Step Process

1. Harvest the pandan leaves.

2. Heat the freshly harvested leaves by boiling them, placing them in a bread oven,
or covering them with hot stones to break down their fibers and accelerate the drying process.

3. After heating, spread the leaves in a sunny area to dry, which should take about a week;
without this initial step, the leaves may take over three weeks and risk developing mildew.

4. When the leaves are tan and papery to the touch, they are ready to be rolled.

5. Roll each leaf around your hand, and straighten it out. Tug or twist the roll tightly,
and once you’ve completed several, tie the tip ends together to hold the rolls in place.

6. Once all the leaves have been rolled and tied into spiral bundles, take a knife and use the back edge of the blade to straighten them.

7. Arrange the strips by length and color (if applicable) to make the weaving process more organized.

8. Start weaving by alternating the strips under and over each other to create the foundation.
Alternate the weaving pattern for each row (over-under for one row, under-over for the next).

9. Use additional strips to braid or stitch the edges of the mat, locking the weave in place.
